CPU Scheduling Algorithms

FCFS (First Come First Serve)

FCFS is a simple, non-preemptive scheduling algorithm that executes processes in the order they arrive. Once a process starts, it runs to completion.

Key Features:

  • FIFO Order: First process to arrive is executed first
  • Non-Preemptive: No interruption once a process starts
  • Convoy Effect: Longer processes delay shorter ones
  • Simple & Fair: Easy to implement but may lead to high waiting times

SJF (Shortest Job First)

Executes the process with the shortest burst time first. Non-preemptive version.

Key Features:

  • Efficient: Minimizes average waiting and turnaround time
  • Risk of Starvation: Long processes may wait indefinitely
  • Optimal for: Batch processing with predictable execution times

SRTF (Shortest Remaining Time First)

Preemptive version of SJF, prioritizes remaining execution time.

Key Features:

  • Highly Responsive: Reduces waiting time for shorter processes
  • Frequent Context Switching: Increases overhead
  • Starvation Risk: Longer processes may rarely get CPU time
  • Best For: Time-sensitive tasks requiring quick execution

Priority Scheduling

Executes processes based on their priority (lower numbers = higher priority).

Key Features:

  • Critical Task Handling: Ensures high-priority completion
  • Starvation Risk: Low-priority processes may wait indefinitely
  • Aging Solution: Gradually increases priority of waiting processes
  • Best For: Real-time systems and critical task scheduling

Round Robin (RR)

Uses time quantum for fair CPU allocation in cyclic manner.

Key Features:

  • Fair Allocation: Ensures no process starvation
  • Time Quantum Critical: Balance between switching and wait time
  • Ideal For: Time-sharing systems and interactive applications
  • Performance: Depends on proper quantum size selection
